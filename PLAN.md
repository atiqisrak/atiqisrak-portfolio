## **7\. Case studies (blog-ready)**

### **Case study 1 — Bento: giving small businesses an AI team they could never hire**

*How a brand-voice wedge turned "another AI tool" into something founders run their business on.*

Small businesses are drowning. They want the output of a company ten times their size and can't afford the headcount — and generic AI gives them copy that sounds like everyone else. The market was loud with "AI for everyone" and short on AI that sounded like *you*.

For Chromatics, Bento was a bet on that exact gap: not another AI wrapper, but brand-voice AI an SMB could trust to speak for them. I owned the wedge — narrowing Bento from "AI does everything" to *AI that learns and protects your voice first* — and scoped ruthlessly: ship the one workflow that earns trust, prove it, expand. I drew the line on where AI talks to a customer and where a human stays in the loop.

The hardest part wasn't the model; it was resisting the pull to make Bento do everything at launch while every team lobbied for their feature. We held the line, launched, and early retention on the core workflow ran roughly 2× the broader feature set — proof the wedge was right.

The lesson I keep relearning: with AI, the model is a commodity. Trust is the product. The first time a founder said Bento "sounded like them," that was the whole game.

### **Case study 2 — Sumo: turning a restaurant's chaos into a system that runs itself**

*POS, cameras, scales, and conveyors wired into one AI nervous system — deployed across campuses, factories, and a telco.*

High-volume kitchens — university canteens, factory cafeterias, a telco's inventory floor — ran on disconnected parts: a POS here, a manual weigh station there, cameras nobody watched, inventory on paper. Money leaked at every seam: billing errors, waste, theft, queues out the door. At thousands of meals a day, a 2% error rate is a real budget line.

I led Sumo as a single nervous system for the restaurant — POS, CC cameras, digital weigh machines, and conveyor belts wired into one AI-watched platform. We deployed across 3 large universities, 2 manufacturing organizations, and the biggest telco's inventory area, sequencing the rollout so each site became the proof for the next.

IoT in the real world is brutal — a scale that drifts, a camera that drops, a belt that jams at lunch rush. The war was reliability: making it trustworthy enough that staff abandoned their paper backup. Once they did, the numbers followed — ~40,000 meals/day processed, billing and queue errors down ~90%, food waste down ~25%.

The flashy part was the hardware. The real moat was a workflow simple enough that a busy line cook would actually use it. Adoption beats integration every time.

### **Case study 3 — AssetIQ: making inventory tell the truth — and unlocking 264% growth**

*RFID + AI turned a guessing game into real-time truth, ended stockouts, and nearly quadrupled revenue.*

The warehouse was guessing. Stock counts took two days and were wrong by the time they finished. Leadership decided on stale data, and "how much do we actually have?" had no fast, trustworthy answer. The hidden cost wasn't just overstock — it was stockouts: every empty shelf was a sale walking out the door.

I led AssetIQ as RFID-driven real-time tracking with an AI analytics layer, built to plug straight into the tools leadership already lived in — Snowflake and Power BI. The bet: don't just track stock, make the data instantly usable where decisions happen.

The hard part was trust, after years of "the system is always wrong." The turning point was the first reconciliation where AssetIQ's count beat the manual one — and was proven right. Accuracy climbed from ~85% to 99.4%, and a two-day stock-count collapsed to about three hours.

Then the real prize showed up. With accurate, real-time inventory, stockouts stopped quietly killing sales — and revenue grew **264%**. The RFID was table stakes; the moment the data appeared in Power BI next to everything else, AssetIQ stopped being "an inventory tool" and became how the business saw itself.
